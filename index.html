States of Matter for Reactants

What is a Reactant and a Product?

  • Reactant: Takes part in and undergoes change during a reaction.
  • Product: Formed as a result of a chemical reaction.

Reactant(s) → Product(s)

Reactions

  • Synthesis: 2 or more elements or compounds combine to form a single compound.
    A + B → AB
  • Single Replacement: An element reacts with a compound and takes the place of another element in the compound.
    A + BC → B + AC
  • Double Replacement: 2 compounds react and the anions and cations of the reactants switch.
    AB + CD → AD + CB
  • Combustion: Involves oxygen and hydrocarbons and produces carbon dioxide and water.
    C xH y + O 2 → H 2O + CO 2

Oxidation & Reduction

  • Oxidation: Complete or partial loss of electrons or gain of oxygen

  • Reduction: Complete or partial gain of electrons or loss of oxygen

  • Redox Reaction: Transfer of oxygen atoms, hydrogen atoms, or electrons.

  • Oxidation Number
    • A number assigned to an element in a chemical that represents the level of oxidation.
    • Rules:
      • The oxidation number of an element in its free state is 0
      • The oxidation number of a monatomic ion is the same as the charge on the ion
      • The sum of all oxidation numbers in a compound is equal to the charge on the ion
      • The oxidation number of an alkali metal in a compound is +1 and for an alkaline Earth metal is +2
      • The oxidation number of oxygen in a compound is -2 except in peroxides where it is -1
      • The oxidation number of hydrogen in a compound is +1, except in binary metal hydrides where it is -1
  • Oxidizing and Reducing Agents:
    • Oxidizing Agent: Reduced and gains electrons
    • Reducing Agent: Oxidized and loses electrons
    • Oxidized: Oxidation number ↑
    • Reduced: Oxidation number ↓
  • Balancing Equations
    • Half Reactions
    • Oxidation Number Change

Volataic/Galvanic Cell

Oxidation occurs at the anode

Reduction occurs at the cathode

Cell Potential

  • Measure of the potential difference between 2 half cells
  • Potential Difference is caused by ability of electrons to flow from one half cell to the other.
  • Eocell = Eoreduction + Eooxidation

Standard Electrode Potential

  • Measure of the individual potential of a reversible electrode.

Electrolytic vs Voltaic Cells

  • Electrolytic Cells: Electrical energy is used to create a forced redox reaction.
  • Voltaic Cells: Energy released by a spontaneous reaction is converted into electrical energy.

Uses of an Electrolytic Cell

  • Recharging Batteries
  • Electroplating Metals

Activity Series

Metal A will replace another metal B in a solution if A is higher in the series.
